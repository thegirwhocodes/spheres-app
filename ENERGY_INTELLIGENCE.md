## Vision

**What if your task manager knew when you'd actually do your best work?**

Energy Intelligence is a science-backed scheduling system that learns your personal energy rhythms and matches tasks to optimal time slots. Instead of treating all hours equally, it recognizes that your brain has natural peaks and valleys—and uses that knowledge to help you accomplish more with less effort.

This isn't just productivity optimization. It's about working *with* your biology, not against it.

---

## The Problem We're Solving

### Traditional scheduling is broken

Most task managers treat time as fungible—any hour is as good as another. But anyone who's tried to do deep analytical work at 3 PM after a heavy lunch knows this isn't true.

**Common pain points:**
- Scheduling important work during your natural energy dips
- Creative blocks because you're trying to brainstorm at the wrong time
- Decision fatigue from making too many choices in the morning
- Exercise routines that fight your body's natural rhythms
- Feeling productive but not actually accomplishing high-priority work

### The cost of ignoring circadian science

Research shows that **time of day accounts for 20% of the variance in cognitive performance**. That means you could be performing 20% worse on tasks simply because of *when* you scheduled them—not because of your abilities, motivation, or preparation.

## The Science Behind Energy Intelligence

### 1. Circadian Rhythms (24-Hour Cycles)

Your body runs on a roughly 24-hour internal clock controlled by the suprachiasmatic nucleus in your brain. This clock regulates:

- **Core body temperature** (peaks in late afternoon)
- **Cortisol levels** (highest in morning, aids alertness)
- **Melatonin production** (rises in evening, induces sleepiness)
- **Cognitive performance** (follows predictable daily patterns)

**Key research findings:**

> "Circadian rhythms act directly on human cognition and indirectly through their fundamental influence on sleep/wake cycles, with the strength of circadian regulation of performance depending on accumulated sleep debt and the cognitive domain."
> — [Annual Reviews: The Circadian Brain and Cognition (2024)](https://www.annualreviews.org/content/journals/10.1146/annurev-psych-022824-043825)

> "Time-of-day effects on basic and more complex cognitive functions depend on an individual's chronotype with a dominant effect of synchrony between chronotype and time of cognitive testing."
> — [PMC: Time of Day and Chronotype in Cognitive Assessment](https://pmc.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/articles/PMC10683050/)

### 2. Ultradian Rhythms (90-120 Minute Cycles)

Within each day, your energy oscillates in shorter cycles discovered by sleep researcher **Nathaniel Kleitman** in the 1950s.

**The 90-minute focus cycle:**
- Your body naturally alternates between higher and lower alertness every 90-120 minutes
- Physiological measures (heart rate, hormones, muscle tension, brain waves) all rise during the active phase
- After ~90 minutes of focused work, your brain needs a 15-20 minute recovery period

**Research evidence:**

> "A groundbreaking study published in the Journal of Cognition showed that professionals who aligned their work with these 90-minute cycles reported 40% higher productivity levels compared to those who worked in random time intervals."
> — [Ahead App: The Science of Time Blocks](https://ahead-app.com/blog/procrastination/the-science-of-time-blocks-why-90-minute-focus-sessions-transform-your-productivity-20241227-203316)

> "Throughout the course of each day, your body moves through a repeating, oscillating energy cycle, rising to an energy peak over the course of an hour and a half to two hours, and then dropping into an energy trough that lasts about twenty minutes."
> — [Blue Zones: Ultradian Rhythms](https://www.bluezones.com/2020/06/how-taking-breaks-can-increase-productivity-boost-energy-levels-and-help-you-show-up-in-your-life/)

### 3. Chronotypes (Your Personal Clock)

Not everyone's internal clock runs on the same schedule. **Chronotype** describes your natural preference for sleep and peak activity times.

| Chronotype | Population | Peak Hours | Characteristics |
|------------|------------|------------|-----------------|
| **Morning Lark** | 15% | 8-11 AM | Wake early, peak before noon, fade by evening |
| **Third Bird** | 65% | 10 AM-12 PM | Typical pattern, morning peak, afternoon dip, evening recovery |
| **Night Owl** | 20% | 4-9 PM | Slow morning start, peak in late afternoon/evening |

**Research findings:**

> "Eighty percent of the population are Morning Larks (15%) and Third Birds (65%) who experience similar energy fluctuations during the day. The other twenty percent of people are Night Owls, who experience an inverse in energy patterns."
> — [Morgen: The Ideal Week for Larks, Owls, and Third Birds](https://www.morgen.so/blog-posts/the-ideal-week-for-larks-owls-and-third-birds)

> "Circadian rhythms — the roughly 24-hour cycles of sleep and wake — are baked into our genes. Even our preferred bedtime is rooted in our DNA."
> — [SRBR: What's a Chronotype?](https://srbr.org/of-larks-and-owls-whats-in-a-chronotype/)

**Important finding on cognitive performance:**

> "Adults who are naturally more active in the evening (eveningness) tended to perform better on cognitive tests than those who are morning people."
> — [Imperial College London (2024)](https://www.imperial.ac.uk/news/254738/being-night-associated-with-mental-sharpness/)

This doesn't mean owls are smarter—it means **synchrony matters**. Owls tested in the evening outperform; larks tested in the morning outperform. The key is matching task timing to your chronotype.

### 4. Decision Fatigue & Ego Depletion

Your capacity for high-quality decisions degrades throughout the day.

**The famous judge study:**

> "At the beginning of the day a judge was likely to give a favorable ruling approximately 65 percent of the time, and as the morning progressed the judge experienced decision fatigue from making so many decisions with the likelihood of favorable rulings trending towards zero. After a lunch break, the judge would return to the courtroom rested and the likelihood of a favorable ruling went back to about 65 percent."
> — [The Decision Lab: Decision Fatigue](https://thedecisionlab.com/biases/decision-fatigue)

**Implications for scheduling:**
- Front-load important decisions to morning hours
- Schedule routine/low-stakes work for afternoon
- Take breaks to restore decision-making capacity

### 5. Cognitive Load Theory

Different tasks require different amounts of mental energy:

| Load Level | Task Type | Examples |
|------------|-----------|----------|
| **High (0.8-1.0)** | Deep Work | Coding, writing, analysis, strategic planning |
| **Medium (0.5-0.7)** | Creative Work | Brainstorming, design, ideation |
| **Low (0.2-0.4)** | Shallow Work | Email, admin, routine tasks |
| **Variable (0.3-0.6)** | Meetings | Depends on type and your role |

**Key insight for creative work:**

> "You don't want to schedule creative time during your most productive time; you want to schedule it when you're slightly less goal-oriented and sharp, when you're on the downward slope and you've started to fatigue, so that you're open to different ways of thinking and creating."
> — [It's Nice That: Finding Your Most Creative Time](https://www.itsnicethat.com/news/sahar-yousef-most-creative-time-of-day-opinion-090320)

This counterintuitive finding is crucial: **slight fatigue actually helps creativity** by reducing the brain's tendency to filter out "irrelevant" ideas.

### 6. Exercise Timing Research

When you exercise affects both your workout and your cognitive state:

**Morning exercise:**
- Higher cortisol levels provide natural energy boost
- Improves sleep quality that night
- Accelerates fat loss (visible by week 4 vs week 8 for evening)
- Lower injury risk (body is more careful when not fully warmed up)

**Evening exercise:**
- Peak core body temperature enhances performance
- Better muscle contractility and enzymatic activity
- Higher strength and power output
- Greater muscle gains in studies

> "Both exercise groups experienced significant improvements in body composition, with fat loss observed as early as week 4 in the morning exercise group and week 8 in the evening exercise group... Evening groups gained more muscle mass during the training weeks 13-24."
> — [PMC: Chronobiology of Exercise](https://pmc.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/articles/PMC10214902/)

## Optimal Scheduling Framework

Based on the research, here's how Energy Intelligence matches tasks to times:

### For Most People (Third Birds - 65%)

| Time | Energy State | Best For | Why |
|------|--------------|----------|-----|
| **6-8 AM** | Rising | Morning routine, light planning | Cortisol awakening response |
| **9-11 AM** | Peak | Deep work, complex decisions | Maximum alertness and focus |
| **11 AM-12 PM** | High | Meetings, collaboration | Still sharp, social energy available |
| **12-1 PM** | Declining | Lunch, light tasks | Natural transition period |
| **1-3 PM** | Trough | Email, admin, routine work | Post-lunch dip—don't fight it |
| **3-5 PM** | Recovery | Creative work, brainstorming | Reduced filtering = more ideas |
| **5-7 PM** | Second wind | Exercise, review, planning tomorrow | Body temperature peaks |
| **7-9 PM** | Winding down | Light reading, reflection | Prepare for sleep |

### Adjustments by Chronotype

**Morning Larks:** Shift everything 1-2 hours earlier. Your deep work window is 7-10 AM.

**Night Owls:** Shift everything 2-4 hours later. Your deep work window is 4-8 PM. Accept that mornings are for low-stakes tasks.

## Features in Energy Intelligence

### 1. Energy Profile Onboarding

A startup-style 6-step flow that builds your personal energy model:

1. **Introduction** - Explains the science
2. **Chronotype Quiz** - Identifies your type (lark/bird/owl)
3. **Energy Drawing** - You trace your perceived energy curve
4. **Preferences** - Work hours, exercise timing
5. **Calendar Analysis** - Scans 1-3 years of history to detect patterns
6. **Results** - Shows your personalized profile

### 2. Calendar History Analysis

Analyzes your past calendar events to discover:
- When you actually schedule deep work
- Meeting-heavy days and times
- Exercise patterns
- Task completion times (via event notes)
- Productivity peaks and troughs

All analysis happens **on-device**—no data leaves your Mac.

### 3. Smart Time Block Suggestions

For each unscheduled task, the system:
1. Categorizes it by cognitive load (deep work, creative, shallow, etc.)
2. Identifies optimal time windows based on your energy profile
3. Checks your calendar for availability
4. Generates a suggestion with reasoning
5. Assigns a confidence score

### 4. Drag-and-Drop Scheduling

The scheduling interface shows:
- **Timeline view** with energy level indicators per hour
- **Suggestion cards** that explain why each time was chosen
- **Drag-and-drop** to reschedule to different times
- **Alternative times** if you don't like the suggestion

### 5. Energy Indicators

Throughout the app, you'll see:
- Current energy level based on time of day
- Energy badges on suggested time blocks
- Visual energy bars in the timeline
- Color coding (green = peak, yellow = moderate, orange = trough)

## Privacy & Trust

Energy Intelligence is designed with privacy at its core:

- **Calendar analysis happens entirely on-device**
- No calendar data is ever sent to external servers
- Your energy profile is stored locally (or in your iCloud)
- The AI only receives task content for categorization
- You can delete your profile and all analysis data anytime
